# Saurabh's 5-Day Upper/Lower + Conditioning Program

Personal uploaded training program for SteadyFit demo / evals.

## Weekly Split

- **Mon — Upper A (push emphasis):** Bench press 4×6–8, Overhead press 3×8–10, Lat pulldown 3×10–12, Lateral raises 3×12–15, Triceps pushdown 2×12
- **Tue — Lower A:** Back squat 4×5–8, Romanian deadlift 3×8–10, Walking lunges 3×10/leg, Leg curl 3×12, Calf raises 3×15
- **Wed — Conditioning:** 25–35 min easy Zone-2 bike/walk, or circuit: kettlebell swings 3×15, sled/farmer carries 3×40m, plank 3×45s (keep HR conversational)
- **Thu — Upper B (pull emphasis):** Chest-supported row 4×8–10, Incline dumbbell press 3×8–12, Face pulls 3×15, Dumbbell curls 3×10–12, Overhead triceps extension 2×12
- **Fri — Lower B:** Front squat or goblet squat 3×8–10, Hip thrust 4×8–12, Bulgarian split squat 3×8/leg, Leg press calf raise 3×15, Plank 3×45s
- **Sat / Sun:** Rest or easy walk 20–30 min

## Progressive Overload Rules

- Add 2.5–5 kg when you hit the top of the rep range on all working sets with 1–2 RIR.
- If form breaks, keep the load and reduce reps before adding weight.
- Log every working set; deload if life stress spikes for 2+ weeks.
- Conditioning day stays easy — do not turn Wed into another hard strength session.

## Deload Week

Every 6th week is a **deload**:

1. Cut working sets by ~40% (e.g. 4 sets → 2 sets).
2. Keep the same exercises; drop load to ~60–70% of recent working weight.
3. Cap strength sessions at 35 minutes; Conditioning becomes an easy 20-min walk.
4. Skip optional accessories (lateral raises, curls, calves) if recovery is poor.
5. Sleep and protein stay the same — this is recovery, not a diet break.

## Travel / Hotel Variant

When traveling, swap Mon/Thu upper days for:

- Push-ups 3×AMRAP, Band rows 3×15, Pike push-ups 3×8–12, Plank 3×45s  
Keep sessions ≤20 minutes. Prefer hotel bodyweight over skipping the day. Wed Conditioning can stay as a hotel-room walk or bodyweight circuit.

## Notes

- Primary goal in this file: fat loss while keeping upper-body strength (5 sessions/week).
- Prefer machines over free weights when knee feels puffy (see personal_notes.md).
