## Beginner Physiology

New trainees experience rapid strength gains in the first 6–12 weeks primarily through **neural adaptation** — the nervous system learns to recruit motor units more efficiently, not because muscle tissue has grown significantly. This means:

- Strength can increase 20–40% in 8 weeks with minimal hypertrophy
- Muscles, tendons, and connective tissue take longer to adapt than strength — **don't let early strength gains convince you to progress load too fast**
- Soreness (DOMS) is expected and normal in weeks 1–3; not a useful marker of progress

## Training Principles for Beginners

```yaml
frequency: "2–3 sessions per week (full body)"
volume: "Low-to-moderate: 2–3 sets per exercise, 8–15 reps"
intensity: "Leave 3–4 reps in reserve (RIR); never training to failure initially"
progression: "Linear: add small weight each session (~1–2kg lower body, ~0.5–1kg upper body)"
program_type: "Full body every session — not split training"
session_duration: "30–45 minutes maximum"
rest_between_sets: "90–120 seconds"
recovery: "At least 1 rest day between sessions"
```

**Why full body for beginners:**
- Practices movements 3x/week vs 1x/week (splits) — faster skill acquisition
- Lower per-session volume = manageable soreness
- Missing one session doesn't mean a muscle group goes untrained for a week

## Recommended Starter Program (3 days/week, Full Body)

### Session A
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Goblet Squat | 3 × 10 | Learn squat pattern with counterbalance |
| Push-Up (or Incline Push-Up) | 3 × max | To 3–4 RIR |
| Dumbbell RDL | 3 × 10 | Hip hinge pattern |
| Assisted Pull-Up or Lat Pulldown | 3 × 10 | Vertical pull |
| Plank | 3 × 30s | Core stability |

### Session B
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dumbbell Goblet Squat | 3 × 12 | Slightly more reps |
| Dumbbell Shoulder Press | 3 × 10 | |
| Single-Leg RDL (light) | 3 × 8/side | Balance and stability |
| Seated Cable Row or Band Row | 3 × 12 | Horizontal pull |
| Dead Bug | 3 × 10/side | Anti-extension core |

Alternate A and B: Mon A / Wed B / Fri A, then Mon B / Wed A / Fri B.

## Progression Protocol
```yaml
rule: "Add weight only when you complete all sets and reps with 3+ RIR"
lower_body_increment: "2.5kg per session when possible"
upper_body_increment: "1.25kg per session when possible"
if_stuck_3_sessions: "Repeat weight; check sleep, nutrition, stress before changing program"
deload_every: "Every 6–8 weeks, reduce volume by 40% for 1 week"
```

## Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

```yaml
mistake_1:
  mistake: "Training to failure every set"
  consequence: "Excessive soreness, poor form, higher injury risk"
  fix: "Leave 3–4 reps in reserve; failure is an advanced tool"

mistake_2:
  mistake: "Copying advanced split programs"
  consequence: "Insufficient frequency per muscle; not optimal for beginners"
  fix: "Full body 3x/week for first 3–6 months"

mistake_3:
  mistake: "Skipping warm-up"
  consequence: "Injury risk; poor movement quality"
  fix: "5–10 min: light cardio + bodyweight versions of the day's exercises"

mistake_4:
  mistake: "Neglecting sleep and nutrition"
  consequence: "70% of beginner gains happen in recovery, not in the gym"
  fix: "7–9 hours sleep; protein 1.6g/kg/day minimum"

mistake_5:
  mistake: "Program hopping after 2 weeks"
  consequence: "Never gives adaptation time; the 'boredom' phase is when adaptation occurs"
  fix: "Run the same program 8–12 weeks before evaluating"
```

## Nutrition Basics for Beginners

```yaml
protein:
  target: "1.6–2.0 g/kg bodyweight/day"
  why: "Muscle protein synthesis requires amino acid availability"
  sources: [chicken, eggs, Greek yogurt, paneer, lentils, tofu, fish]

calories:
  goal_fat_loss: "200–300 kcal daily deficit (slow and sustainable)"
  goal_muscle: "100–200 kcal surplus"
  goal_recomposition: "Maintenance calories + high protein (works well for beginners)"

hydration:
  target: "3L+ per day; more on training days"
  note: "Dehydration reduces strength output by up to 5%"

meal_timing:
  pre_workout: "Meal 1–2 hours before; carbs + protein"
  post_workout: "Within 2 hours; protein + carbs (timing matters less than daily total)"
```
