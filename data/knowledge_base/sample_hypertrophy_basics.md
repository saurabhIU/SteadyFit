# Hypertrophy programming basics

## Weekly volume for beginners

Most evidence points to roughly 10-20 hard sets per muscle group per week for
muscle growth. Beginners should start at the LOW end (about 10 sets) and add
volume only when progress stalls. More is not better if recovery suffers.

## Progressive overload

Aim to do slightly more over time: an extra rep, a small load increase, or an
extra set. Track lifts; if a lift has not improved in 3-4 weeks, change one
variable (volume, exercise selection, or recovery).

## Training when under-recovered

After poor sleep (under ~6 hours) or high stress, reduce load 10-20 percent or
cut a set from each exercise rather than skipping entirely. Consistency at
lower intensity beats sporadic maximal sessions.

## Missed sessions

A missed session is a scheduling event, not a failure. Do not "double up" the
next day; redistribute the week and keep total volume the same or lower.
